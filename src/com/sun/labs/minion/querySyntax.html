Term Operators

There are a number of operators that affect how and what terms are retrieved from the index for a term in the query. These operators may be applied to a term in any order, and their effect is cumulative.

<morph>

Inidicates that the engine should consider all known morphological variants for the term when retrieving terms from the index. This is the default term operator in the standard engine configuration.

For example, the query:

<morph> dog
will retrieve documents containing the terms dog, dogs, dogging and dogged.

<exact>

When the <exact> operator is applied to a term, the engine will only retrieve that exact form of the term from the index. For example, the query:

<exact> dog
will only retrieve documents containing the term dog. It will not retrieve documents that contain variants of the term such as dogs, dogging and dogged.
